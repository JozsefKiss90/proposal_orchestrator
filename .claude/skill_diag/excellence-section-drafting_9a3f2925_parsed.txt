      "content": "MAESTRO (Multi-Agent Ecosystem for Strategic Task Reasoning and Orchestration) addresses a fundamental bottleneck in European AI deployment: the absence of AI agent architectures capable of autonomous, robust, and trustworthy operation in complex real-world tasks that require long-horizon planning, persistent coherent memory, decentralised coordination among multiple agents, and principled integration of external tools and APIs. Today's AI agents \u2014 while capable in constrained settings \u2014 struggle with long-horizon planning stability, lose coherence over extended interactions, fail silently in novel situations, and lack principled mechanisms for integrating external tools and APIs into their reasoning and action loops. These limitations prevent the deployment of autonomous AI systems in safety-critical, high-value European sectors including healthcare and advanced manufacturing. MAESTRO directly responds to HORIZON-CL4-2026-05-DIGITAL-EMERGING-02 by developing next-generation AI agents powered by large language models capable of interpreting high-level instructions, generating structured plans, using tools, and performing actions autonomously to achieve specified goals (SR-01). Target: at least 40% improvement in plan success rate over pure LLM-based agent baselines (ReAct, Reflexion) on standardised agent benchmarks (WebArena, SWE-bench, GAIA) for tasks requiring more than 10 sequential steps, achieved by month 36.\n\nOBJ-2 (Unified adaptive memory architecture; WP3, BIIS lead): Design and implement a tri-store memory architecture integrating working memory, episodic memory, and semantic memory stores with a learned memory controller governing information flow between stores.
