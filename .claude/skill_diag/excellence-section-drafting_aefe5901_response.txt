      "content": "MAESTRO (Multi-Agent Ecosystem for Strategic Task Reasoning and Orchestration) addresses a fundamental structural gap in the state of the art: the fragmentation of three active AI research communities — LLM-based autonomous agents, classical planning and multi-agent systems, and memory-augmented neural architectures — whose individual advances cannot, in isolation, produce agents that are robust, trustworthy, and deployable in safety-relevant real-world settings. The project's central thesis is that planning, memory, and coordination capabilities must be co-designed around a shared architectural substrate to deliver the expected outcomes of the call: significant improvements in AI agent autonomy, robustness, and reliability (EO-01), and innovative multi-agent frameworks demonstrating effective decentralised coordination beyond the capabilities of individual agents (EO-02).\n\nMAESTRO pursues seven measurable, verifiable objectives structured across three research pillars, a cross-cutting orchestration capability, and three sector validation demonstrators within a 48-month duration:\n\nOBJ-1 — Neuro-Symbolic Planning Engine (Lead: ATU, Month 36): Develop a hybrid planning engine combining MCTS and HTN planning with LLM-based heuristic evaluation, decomposing high-level goals into formally specified sub-task graphs with pre-conditions, post-conditions, and verification checkpoints. Target: ≥40% improvement in plan success rate over LLM-baseline architectures (ReAct, Reflexion) on standardised benchmarks (WebArena, SWE-bench, GAIA) for tasks requiring more than 10 sequential steps.\n\nOBJ-2 — Adaptive Memory Architecture (Lead: BIIS, Month 30): Implement a tri-store memory architecture integrating working, episodic, and semantic memory with a learned controller governing information flow, retrieval, and forgetting. Target: ≥30% improvement in task coherence and factual consistency over context-window-only baselines on interaction sequences exceeding 50 agent steps on MAESTRO's long-horizon evaluation suite.\n\nOBJ-3 — Multi-Agent Coordination Protocols (Lead: CERIA, Month 36): Develop formally specified task-delegation and negotiation protocols with provable convergence guarantees and auditable interaction traces. Target: ≥25% improvement in joint task completion over independent agent baselines for coordination of five or more heterogeneous agents, with formal convergence proof.\n\nOBJ-8 — External Tool and API Orchestration Layer (Lead: ATU, Month 36): Develop a typed tool registry supporting 30 or more tools and APIs across all demonstrator domains, with formal selection policies, standardised adapters for heterogeneous protocols (HL7 FHIR, OPC-UA, REST, GraphQL), post-invocation verification, and full provenance traces. Targets: ≥95% tool invocation success rate, mean recovery time of five seconds or less from tool failure, provenance coverage for 100% of tool invocations.\n\nOBJ-4 — Healthcare Demonstrator (Lead: NIHS, Month 42): Validate the MAESTRO framework in a clinical decision support system, achieving diagnostic accuracy within 10% of specialist clinician performance on 500 de-identified clinical cases spanning five diagnostic domains, validated by clinical experts at NIHS.\n\nOBJ-5 — Manufacturing Demonstrator (Lead: ISIA, Month 42): Validate the MAESTRO framework in process optimisation at ISIA's sensor-instrumented pilot factory. Targets: ≥15% reduction in defect rates and ≥10% reduction in energy consumption over a three-month operational validation period.\n\nOBJ-6 — Logistics Transfer Demonstrator (Lead: ELI, Month 42): Validate cross-sector generalisability through a logistics and supply chain demonstrator. Classical planning and multi-agent systems deliver formal guarantees but cannot handle the open-ended ambiguity of real-world natural language tasks. Memory-augmented neural systems advance retrieval augmentation for single-turn accuracy but are not integrated with planning and coordination. No existing system integrates all three capabilities with principled external tool and API integration, despite the call's explicit requirement (SR-04) that such integration is essential for overcoming large model limitations in accuracy-critical applications.\n\nMAESTRO's scientific ambition is the co-design of these capabilities into a unified architecture with coherent formal properties none of the three communities can achieve individually. The neuro-symbolic planning engine provides verifiable sub-task decomposition and proven plan recovery from intermediate states absent from pure LLM systems. The multi-agent coordination layer delivers formally specified protocols with convergence guarantees, in contrast to ad-hoc LLM agent communication. The External Tool and API Orchestration Layer is an architecturally central research contribution — a typed tool capability model with formal selection policies integrated into the planning engine and auditable execution traces stored in adaptive memory — distinguishing MAESTRO from open-source agent frameworks that treat tool use as a function-calling interface.\n\nR&I Maturity Positioning\n\nMAESTRO is positioned at the foundational research end of the spectrum, consistent with TRL 2 at project start, developing neuro-symbolic planning formalisms, tri-store memory controllers, and convergence-guaranteed coordination protocols as novel architectural concepts.       "content": "Concepts, Models, and Assumptions\n\nMAESTRO's methodology is grounded in the co-design principle: all three research pillars and the External Tool and API Orchestration Layer are developed from the outset with shared data representations, formal interfaces, and integration protocols, ensuring that the assembled system exhibits emergent formal properties not achievable by any individual component. The methodology rests on three core assumptions: (i) neuro-symbolic integration can combine LLM representational flexibility with the formal guarantees of symbolic planning without sacrificing either; (ii) a tri-store memory architecture managed by a learned controller can provide long-horizon agent coherence at acceptable computational cost; and (iii) formally specified delegation and negotiation protocols with provable convergence guarantees can produce multi-agent behaviour that is simultaneously efficient, robust, and auditable in operational deployment settings.\n\nPillar 1 — Neuro-Symbolic Planning and Reasoning (WP2, Lead: ATU)\n\nWP2 delivers the hybrid planning engine through three sequential tasks. T2-01 develops sub-task graph decomposition: natural language goal specifications are parsed into structured sub-task graphs with formally specified pre-conditions, post-conditions, and verification checkpoints using a hybrid LLM-symbolic pipeline combining neural language understanding with symbolic constraint enforcement. T2-02 integrates search-based algorithms: MCTS explores open-ended solution spaces while HTN provides hierarchical decomposition; LLM-based heuristic evaluation guides search without compromising symbolic constraint enforcement or completeness guarantees. T2-03 implements plan monitoring and self-correction, with a runtime monitor detecting execution failures at each verification checkpoint and triggering replanning from the most recent verified intermediate state, eliminating the error accumulation over long task horizons that characterises pure LLM action loops. Deliverables: D2-01 architecture specification (Month 18) and D2-02 planning engine software prototype (Month 36). This directly addresses SR-02 and EO-01.\n\nPillar 2 — Adaptive Memory Architecture (WP3, Lead: BIIS)\n\nWP3 develops the tri-store memory system through three tasks. T3-01 designs the architecture: working memory is a structured, attention-managed short-term store maintaining current goals, active plan state, and recent observations; episodic memory is a retrieval-augmented store of past task episodes indexed by context similarity, enabling experience-based agent behaviour without model retraining; semantic memory is a persistent knowledge graph connecting domain concepts, tool capabilities, and learned task patterns. T3-02 implements neural dense retrieval for episodic memory lookup, building on BIIS's expertise in retrieval-augmented generation, and graph-embedding methods for semantic memory queries. T3-03 develops forgetting mechanisms and a learned controller balancing recall accuracy against computational overhead, governing information flow across all three stores. Deliverables: D3-01 design specification (Month 12) and D3-02 adaptive memory system software (Month 30). This addresses SR-03 and directly enables the long-horizon coherence required by EO-01.\n\nPillar 3 — Decentralised Multi-Agent Coordination (WP4, Lead: CERIA)\n\nWP4 develops formal multi-agent coordination protocols through three tasks. T4-01 designs the task-delegation protocol using mechanism design and game theory: agents decompose tasks, negotiate subtask assignments, and establish commitment structures with formally proven convergence, building on CERIA's ERC Advanced Grant results in formal methods for multi-agent systems. T4-02 develops conflict resolution under partial observability: agents with inconsistent world models or contested resources employ game-theoretic negotiation mechanisms with formal correctness guarantees under bounded adversarial conditions. T4-03 implements the auditable interaction trace module: every delegation step, negotiation exchange, and joint decision produces a structured, human-readable record stored in the adaptive memory architecture (T3-02), enabling post-hoc inspection and regulatory auditability in the healthcare and manufacturing deployment contexts. Deliverables: D4-01 protocol specification (Month 18) and D4-02 decentralised coordination software (Month 36). This addresses SR-05(c) and directly delivers EO-02.\n\nCross-Cutting Capability — External Tool and API Orchestration Layer\n\nIntegrated architecturally across WP2, WP3, and WP4, the External Tool and API Orchestration Layer addresses SR-04 as a core methodological commitment rather than a peripheral add-on. A typed tool registry with machine-readable capability models is queried by the planning engine (T2-02) during tool selection decisions; standardised API adapters abstract HL7 FHIR (healthcare demonstrator, WP5), OPC-UA industrial connectors (manufacturing demonstrator, WP6), and logistics tracking and scheduling APIs (WP7) into a uniform invocation interface; post-invocation verification validates tool outputs against expected schemas before incorporation into agent state; execution monitoring with timeout detection and automated fallback is integrated with the T2-03 self-correction capability; and full provenance traces for every tool invocation are stored in episodic memory (T3-02) to support experience-based tool-selection learning and multi-agent delegation accountability.\n\nDemonstrator Validation Methodology (WP5, WP6, WP7)\n\nAll three demonstrators follow a three-stage validation sequence co-designed with industry partners. Stage one establishes measurable baselines: use case requirements definition and KPI framework design (T5-01, T6-01, T7-01) is completed with domain-expert partners before any MAESTRO system is deployed, ensuring performance assessment is grounded in operational reference conditions. Stage two deploys the integrated system with domain-specific protocols and operational data: T5-02 at NIHS with HL7 FHIR integration and specialist sub-agent deployment against de-identified clinical data; T6-02 at ISIA's pilot factory with digital twin and OPC-UA integration and multi-agent coordination deployment across production line segments; T7-02 across ELI's live supply chain corridors with agent-based planning and negotiation operating on real logistics operations data. Stage three benchmarks performance: expert evaluation against operational baselines and current state-of-the-art agent systems (T5-03, T6-03, T7-03) produces validated performance evidence for all applicable objectives. SR-06 compliance — quantitative KPIs, standardised benchmarking, structured progress monitoring — is confirmed at 6-month integration checkpoints using the WP8 cross-cutting evaluation framework.\n\nIntegration and Benchmarking (WP8, Lead: FIIT)\n\nT8-01 develops the cross-cutting evaluation framework and KPI definitions across all research pillars and demonstrators. T8-02 integrates the three pillar software outputs (D2-02, D3-02, D4-02) into the unified MAESTRO framework. T8-03 produces the MAESTRO benchmark suite (D8-02, Month 42) as a publicly released community resource, leveraging FIIT's AI evaluation methodology expertise and EDIH network connections. The final integration and evaluation report (D8-03, Month 48) provides the definitive scientific assessment of the unified system against all project objectives.\n\nLinks to National and International R&I Activities\n\nMAESTRO connects to the European Partnership on AI, Data and Robotics (ADRA) through dedicated cohesion tasks in WP1 (T1-03) and maintains coordinated cohesion activity with the GenAI4EU central Hub (CSA HORIZON-CL4-2025-03-HUMAN-18). The project builds on prior EU-funded work within the consortium: ATU's prior Horizon Europe contribution to the AI-on-demand platform; NIHS's participation in the European Health Data Space pilot implementation; FIIT's H2020 AI-on-demand contribution; and BIIS's Horizon Europe project on unified transformers for text and retrieval. AI Factories and Data Labs are linked for compute-intensive LLM training and evaluation (SR-11). All communicable results are shared through the AI-on-demand platform (SR-10) and demonstrator results are validated in Testing and Experiment Facilities and disseminated through European Digital Innovation Hubs.\n\nInterdisciplinary Approach\n\nMAESTRO integrates six distinct disciplinary traditions with direct methodological consequences for the core architecture. Neuro-symbolic AI and formal automated planning (ATU, CERIA) contributes the HTN and MCTS planning formalisms, mechanism design frameworks, and convergence proofs. Memory-augmented neural networks and neural information retrieval (BIIS) contributes the dense retrieval and knowledge graph methods underlying the episodic and semantic memory components. Clinical informatics and health data integration (NIHS) ensures the healthcare demonstrator addresses real clinical workflow constraints, HL7 FHIR interoperability standards, and clinical expert validation requirements. Industrial AI, digital twin modelling, and manufacturing process engineering (ISIA) ensures the manufacturing demonstrator operates under realistic factory timing and OPC-UA data integration constraints. LLM engineering and production-grade AI deployment (BAL) bridges research prototypes to deployable open-source software components. AI evaluation methodology and technology transfer (FIIT) ensures research results are measured against reproducible benchmarks and reached by industry through EDIH networks. The co-design architecture is the direct methodological expression of this interdisciplinary integration: WP2–WP4 formal specifications were developed collectively across partner institutions to ensure composability rather than post-hoc integration.\n\nSocial Sciences and Humanities\n\nMAESTRO's primary research contributions are technical, addressing computational architectures for AI agents. Dedicated SSH disciplinary contributions are not a primary design element of the core research pillars. Ethics, accountability, and transparency considerations are embedded through WP1 ethics compliance oversight (T1-03), explainability and auditability requirements in the clinical (WP5) and manufacturing (WP6) demonstrators, and FIIT's responsible AI assessment expertise applied throughout the WP8 evaluation framework. These contributions are proportionate to the primarily technical nature of the research topic.\n\nGender Dimension in Research and Innovation Content\n\nSex and gender stratification will be incorporated into the healthcare clinical demonstrator (WP5): the benchmark dataset of 500 de-identified clinical cases (T5-01) will be stratified by sex and gender, and the T5-03 performance analysis will evaluate diagnostic reasoning outcomes across demographic groups to ensure no systematic sex- or gender-based disparities are introduced by the AI system. The consortium will apply gender-balanced recruitment principles in the appointment of researchers across all work packages.\n\nOpen Science Practices\n\nMAESTRO commits to a comprehensive open science approach aligned with SR-10 and SR-11. The unified MAESTRO agent framework is released as open-source software (D9-02, Month 36, Lead: BAL), enabling the European research and developer community to build on the project's results. All models, benchmarks, and evaluation results are contributed to the AI-on-demand platform (OBJ-7, Month 48). The MAESTRO benchmark suite (D8-02, Month 42) is made freely available as a community resource for AI agent evaluation research. These practices maximise durable scientific and societal impact, lower barriers for European SMEs and startups building on next-generation AI agent technology, and strengthen European AI research infrastructure.\n\nData Management Plan\n\nA comprehensive Data Management Plan (D1-02, Month 6, Lead: ATU) will apply FAIR principles (Findable, Accessible, Interoperable, Reusable) to all project datasets, including de-identified clinical records from NIHS, manufacturing operational data from ISIA's pilot factory, and logistics operational data from ELI.
