The methodology implements the call's requirement for KPI-based progress assessment (SR-06) through structured 6-month integration checkpoints defined in the project management framework (WP1, D1-01) and the cross-cutting evaluation framework (WP8, T8-01).\n\nWP2 (Neuro-Symbolic Planning and Reasoning Engine, lead: ATU) develops the hybrid planning engine through three tasks. T2-01 designs executable sub-task graphs with formally specified pre-conditions, post-conditions, and verification checkpoints. T2-02 integrates Monte Carlo Tree Search and hierarchical task network planning algorithms with LLM-driven heuristic evaluation — using LLMs as heuristic evaluators within the symbolic search process rather than as plan generators, a critical methodological choice that preserves formal guarantees while leveraging LLM generalisability. T2-03 implements plan monitoring and self-correction, enabling replanning from verified intermediate states upon execution failure rather than restarting from scratch, directly addressing the fragility of chain-of-thought-based planning over long task horizons. Key deliverables: D2-01 (neuro-symbolic planning architecture specification, Month 18) and D2-02 (planning engine software prototype, Month 36).\n\nWP3 (Adaptive Memory Architecture, lead: BIIS) develops the unified tri-store memory system. T3-01 designs the architecture of working memory (structured short-term store maintaining current goals, active plan state, and recent observations), episodic memory (retrieval-augmented store of past task episodes indexed by context similarity), and semantic memory (persistent knowledge graph connecting domain concepts, tool capabilities, and task patterns). T3-02 implements memory indexing and retrieval strategies including context-similarity episodic retrieval and knowledge graph traversal for semantic memory. T3-03 develops forgetting mechanisms and coherence maintenance via a learned memory controller that manages information lifecycle to prevent context contamination over long agent interactions, addressing the key challenge of balancing recall accuracy against computational cost in real-time operation. Key deliverables: D3-01 (memory architecture design specification, Month 12) and D3-02 (adaptive memory system software, Month 30).\n\nWP4 (Decentralised Multi-Agent Coordination, lead: CERIA) develops formal coordination protocols. T4-01 designs the task-delegation and negotiation protocol with formal specifications and provable convergence guarantees. T4-02 develops conflict resolution mechanisms for partial observability settings where agent world models may be inconsistent and resources are contested. T4-03 implements the auditable multi-agent interaction trace module, ensuring every delegation step, negotiation, and joint decision produces a structured trace that human operators can inspect. CERIA's expertise in formal verification of multi-agent protocols provides the methodological foundation for embedded invariant verification, ensuring convergence guarantees are proven rather than assumed. Key deliverables: D4-01 (multi-agent coordination protocol specification, Month 18) and D4-02 (decentralised coordination software, Month 36).\n\nEach demonstrator work package (WP5–WP7) begins with co-designed use case and KPI framework definition with sector-leading industry partners (T5-01, T6-01, T7-01) before any system development, ensuring validation against operationally meaningful criteria. WP5 (Healthcare Demonstrator, lead: NIHS) deploys the integrated MAESTRO framework with HL7 FHIR integration (T5-02) for autonomous patient data gathering, differential diagnosis planning, and specialist sub-agent coordination, validated by clinical experts against diagnostic benchmarks (T5-03) using NIHS's ethics-approved de-identified clinical data repository. WP6 (Manufacturing Demonstrator, lead: ISIA) integrates OPC-UA industrial connectors and digital twin APIs (T6-02) and conducts operational pilot factory validation (T6-03) across ISIA's Industry 4.0 production lines, measuring performance against defect rate and energy consumption KPIs. WP7 (Logistics Transfer Demonstrator, lead: ELI) deploys agent-based planning and negotiation across ELI's international logistics corridors (T7-02, T7-03), using disruption recovery and schedule adherence benchmarks to demonstrate MAESTRO's architectural portability beyond the call's minimum sector requirement.\n\nWP8 (Integration, Evaluation and Benchmarking, lead: FIIT) defines a cross-cutting evaluation framework and KPI suite applicable across all three demonstrators (T8-01, D8-01 Month 12), integrates all MAESTRO research pillars into the unified system (T8-02), and produces a community-facing standardised benchmark suite (T8-03, D8-02 Month 42). National and international R&I activities feeding into the project include the European Partnership on AI, Data and Robotics (ADRA) — to which MAESTRO allocates dedicated cohesion tasks (WP1, T1-03) — and the GenAI4EU central Hub (CSA HORIZON-CL4-2025-03-HUMAN-18). Relevant prior work from consortium partners includes H2020 and HE projects on trustworthy AI and AI-on-demand platform contributions (ATU), multi-agent coordination and formal methods (CERIA), knowledge infrastructure and unified transformers for retrieval (BIIS), and European Health Data Space pilots (NIHS).\n\nMAESTRO integrates expertise from five disciplinary streams: (1) artificial intelligence and machine learning, including formal methods, automated planning, neural architectures, and LLM engineering (ATU, CERIA, BIIS, BAL); (2) clinical informatics and medical data science, including HL7 FHIR health data integration and clinical validation methodology (NIHS); (3) mechanical and manufacturing engineering including industrial IoT, digital twins, and predictive maintenance (ISIA); (4) logistics and operations research including multi-modal transport planning and supply chain optimisation (ELI); and (5) AI evaluation, benchmarking, and technology transfer through EDIH networks (FIIT).
