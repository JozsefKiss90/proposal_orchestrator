This section describes the contribution of this specific project \u2014 not AI agent R&I in general.\n\nContribution to expected outcomes. The call specifies two expected outcomes. EO-01 requires significant improvements in the autonomy, robustness, and reliability of AI agents through advanced planning mechanisms, memory management, and reasoning capabilities. MAESTRO addresses this through three interoperable architectural outputs. First, the ATU-led neuro-symbolic planning engine (WP2, D2-02, M36) integrates hierarchical task networks and Monte Carlo Tree Search with LLM-based heuristics and verifiable sub-task graph decompositions with formal pre- and post-conditions, advancing agent autonomy and robustness beyond current chain-of-thought approaches by enabling verifiable plan monitoring and self-correction (T2-03, WP2). Second, the BIIS-led adaptive tri-store memory architecture (WP3, D3-02, M30) combines working, episodic, and semantic memory stores with a learned controller, enabling agents to maintain coherence across extended interaction sequences without retraining \u2014 directly addressing the reliability failures arising from LLM context-window limitations in long-horizon deployments. Third, the FIIT-led evaluation framework and benchmark suite (WP8, D8-01 M12, D8-02 M42) produces standardised, reproducible baselines quantifying improvements in planning, memory, and multi-agent coordination across task categories, enabling the field to measure the improvements MAESTRO delivers. All three outputs are validated to TRL 5 across three sector-specific demonstrators (WP5, WP6, WP7). EO-02 requires innovative multi-agent frameworks demonstrating effective decentralised coordination and collaboration beyond individual agent capabilities. MAESTRO delivers this through the CERIA-led formally verified multi-agent coordination protocol suite (WP4, D4-01 M18, D4-02 M36), with mathematically proven convergence guarantees, auditable multi-agent interaction traces (T4-03), and formal conflict-resolution mechanisms under partial observability \u2014 validated across the healthcare (WP5), manufacturing (WP6), and logistics (WP7) demonstrators, confirming generalisability to heterogeneous real-world multi-agent deployments.\n\nContribution to expected impacts.
